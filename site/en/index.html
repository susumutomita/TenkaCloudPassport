No business card,

Someone came up to talk after your lightning talk, but you never printed business cards — and you do not want to pay for a paid card service either. What this is not

What this is not.

NOT / 01

Not a paid subscription

This is not a monthly-fee card service. It is free, with no account to expire.

NOT / 02

Not an account sign-up

No sign-up, no login. Enter your name and you can use it right away.

NOT / 03

Not a data custodian

Your Intro Card is saved only on your device. Nothing is sent to a server.

How it works

All you hand over is one QR code.

  1. Create your Intro Card

    Enter your name (required), title, organization, self-introduction, and links. Nothing leaves your device until you explicitly save it.

  2. Show the QR code

    Once saved, your name, self-introduction, and a large, real QR code appear on screen. Just show this screen to the other person.

  3. They scan it with a standard camera

    No app install required. Scanning the QR with an iPhone or Android standard camera opens your Intro Card page in a browser (the other person needs to be online). Adding you to contacts is an optional action inside that page.

  4. The conversation continues

    After reading your self-introduction, the other person picks up the conversation from there. This replaces exchanging business cards with handing over a conversation starter.

Roadmap

Step 1 is implemented. Everything after that is a concept.

  1. Implemented

    Hand off an Intro Card as a QR code

    The other person needs no app, just a standard camera, to open your Intro Card page. Adding you to contacts is an optional action they choose inside that page. This is the feature described on this page.

  2. Concept

    Research the person you received

    A concept where the receiving app automatically researches the other person online and builds an ontology (a structure of who they are, what they do, and what they care about). This requires reversing the current "no external communication" policy, so an ADR will define an opt-in design when work starts.

  3. Concept

    Visualize people nearby

    A concept for visualizing which event attendees are doing something close to what you do.

  4. Concept

    On-device agents find a connection

    A concept where, once the other person also has the app, on-device LLM agents exchange introductions to find a topic that could lead to a follow-up meeting. The existing Pet / Lounge / Bridge assets are expected to return to center stage here.

Steps 2 through 4 are not implemented yet. We list them as concepts and do not describe them as things you can do today.

Privacy by design

Not holding your data is the design.

  • No accountNo sign-up, no login. Your data never touches a server.
  • Everything stays on-deviceYour Intro Card lives only on your device. The only way it leaves is a JSON Backup you export yourself.
  • Sharing is just showing a QR codeThere is no path for receiving someone else's information. You only ever hand over your own card.
  • No telemetryNothing is collected or transmitted. The design requires no server and no account.

Try it

Run it yourself.

git clone https://github.com/susumutomita/TenkaCloudPassport.git
cd TenkaCloudPassport
make install
make start   # scan the QR from Expo Go on your phone

Install Expo Go on your phone, connect it to the same network as your Mac, then scan the QR code shown in the terminal. To try it in a browser instead, run bun run web.

No dev environment handy? Open the Web version (GitHub Pages) directly in your phone's browser. Data is stored in the browser's on-device storage, so switching browsers or devices does not carry your card over, and the first load requires an online connection. On iOS Safari, tap the Share icon and choose "Add to Home Screen"; on Android Chrome, open the menu and choose "Add to Home Screen" or "Install app" to launch it from an icon on your home screen (this is not App Store or Play Store distribution).
