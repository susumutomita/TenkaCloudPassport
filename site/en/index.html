Not a business card

What this is not.

NOT / 01

Not a contact manager

It does not collect contacts. There is no field for name, email, phone, or company.

NOT / 02

Not a social network

No accounts, no follows. When the Lounge ends, all that remains is your memory and the conversation.

NOT / 03

Not a matching app

It does not rank people by score. Your Pet finds exactly one Bridge — an opening line to start with.

How it works

Your Pet builds the bridge. You cross it.

  1. Prepare your Pet

    No account required. Just your Pet's name, an emoji, and conversation material (Topics / Offer / Looking For) chosen from the bundled catalog. Nothing leaves your device until you explicitly save it.

  2. Join a 20-minute Lounge with a QR code

    Scan the Host's one-time invite QR, explicitly confirm the Preview of what you are about to share, and join. Every Lounge ends at 20 minutes, no exceptions.

  3. Pets find one Bridge together

    Only the clues you chose to share are matched, surfacing one Bridge — a topic these two people could start talking about.

  4. The app steps back

    Once the conversation starts, the app's job is done. Lounge data is discarded on whichever comes first: leaving, the Host ending it, or the 20-minute expiry.

Privacy by design

Not holding your data is the design.

  • No accountNo sign-up, no login. Your data never touches a server.
  • Everything stays on-deviceYour Profile lives only on your device. The only way it leaves is a JSON Backup you export yourself.
  • Discarded after 20 minutesLounge data is never persisted; it is discarded at the earliest possible moment.
  • No telemetryNothing is collected or transmitted. AI decisions are designed to run entirely on-device. Today that is handled by a Rules Provider; Local LLM is Planned (not yet in the default branch).

Try it

Run it yourself.

git clone https://github.com/susumutomita/TenkaCloudPassport.git
cd TenkaCloudPassport
make install
make start   # scan the QR from Expo Go on your phone

Install Expo Go on your phone, connect it to the same network as your Mac, then scan the QR code shown in the terminal. To try it in a browser instead, run bun run web.
