Privacy Policy

TenkaCloud Passport is a free Intro Card app for in-person events, provided by independent developer Susumu Tomita. This page explains what data the app handles, and what it does not, all in one place.

Last updated: [owner to fill in at launch]

No account

No sign-up, no login required.

Zero server transmission

There is no server that stores your card data.

On-device only

Drafts stay on your device until you delete them.

No tracking

No analytics, behavioral tracking, or third-party sharing.

Account and data we collect

No account or login is required to use TenkaCloud Passport. Your name, title, organization, self-introduction, and links are never sent anywhere until you explicitly save your card.

Where your data is stored

Your saved Intro Card draft is stored only on your device. There is no export or automatic backup feature, so it never leaves your device until you delete it. Deleting the app, or clearing its on-device data, deletes your card as well.

How sharing works

The QR code you show to share your Intro Card encodes the card's contents only inside the URL fragment (the part after #). When someone scans it, their browser renders that fragment directly — the contents are never sent to any server.

Analytics and tracking

We do not measure usage, track behavior, serve ads, or share data with third parties. The app has no mechanism to send telemetry at all.

Adding a contact

When someone taps "Add to contacts" on your Intro Card page, a vCard (.vcf) file is generated inside their own browser. That happens entirely on their device and never touches a server. For which devices and browsers this has been verified on, see How it works on the home page.

Contact us

Questions or concerns can be raised via GitHub Issues. If a dedicated contact channel is set up later, this page will be updated accordingly.
